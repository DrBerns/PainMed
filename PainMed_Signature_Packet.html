Notice of Privacy Practices

HIPAA Privacy Notice

How your medical information may be used and disclosed, and how you can access it.
Version v_04.13.26

This notice describes how medical information about you may be used and disclosed and how you can get access to this information. Please review it carefully.

Our Pledge Regarding Your Health Information

We are required by federal and Florida law to maintain the privacy of your protected health information (PHI), to provide you with this Notice of our legal duties and privacy practices, and to follow the terms of the Notice currently in effect. We reserve the right to change this Notice and to make the revised Notice effective for all PHI we maintain. A current copy of this Notice will be posted in our offices and available on request.

How We May Use and Disclose Your Health Information Without Your Authorization

Treatment. We may use and disclose your PHI to provide, coordinate, or manage your healthcare · for example, sharing records with a referring physician, hospital, imaging center, or specialist involved in your care.

Payment. We may use and disclose your PHI to bill and collect payment from you, your insurance company (including auto/PIP carriers), or other responsible third parties involved in payment for your care, including attorneys when applicable to your case.

Healthcare Operations. We may use and disclose your PHI for quality assessment, staff training, credentialing, licensing, audits, legal services, and other business activities necessary to run our practice.

Appointment Reminders & Health-Related Communications. We may contact you by phone, text, email, or mail regarding appointments, treatment alternatives, or other health-related information. These communications may not be secure.

As Required or Permitted by Law. We may use or disclose your PHI without your authorization when required by court order, public health activities, workers' compensation, law enforcement, or health oversight agencies.

All other uses and disclosures require your written authorization, which you may revoke at any time in writing. We will never sell your PHI or use it for marketing without your authorization.

Your Rights Regarding Your Health Information

You have the right to inspect and copy your health information, request amendments, request an accounting of disclosures, request restrictions, request confidential communications, receive a paper copy of this Notice, and be notified of any breach of your unsecured PHI.

How to File a Complaint

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with our Privacy Officer or with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Office for Civil Rights (www.hhs.gov/ocr).

Terms of Acceptance

Terms of Acceptance & Consent for Care

Scope of care, examination, imaging, risks, and consent to treatment.
Version v_04.13.26

Scope of Care

PainMed provides evaluation and treatment of neuromusculoskeletal conditions through chiropractic care, physical medicine, active and passive rehabilitation, and massage therapy. If during your evaluation we identify a condition outside our scope of practice, we may recommend referral to an appropriate provider.

Examination, Diagnostic Imaging, and Reassessment

Your care may include physical examination, orthopedic and neurological testing, range-of-motion assessment, and diagnostic imaging when clinically indicated. Reassessment and repeat imaging may be recommended based on clinical judgment.

Risks of Care

Chiropractic / manual care: temporary soreness, stiffness, bruising, muscle strain, soft-tissue injury, aggravation of existing condition; rarely · disc injury, fracture, nerve irritation, or stroke from cervical manipulation (vertebrobasilar injury). Rehab & modalities: soreness, temporary symptom increase, skin irritation, rare allergic reactions. No guarantees have been made regarding outcomes. X-ray: exposure to ionizing radiation. If pregnant or possibly pregnant, inform clinical staff before any imaging.

Diagnostic Imaging Ownership

X-ray and other diagnostic images are the property of PainMed and are part of your medical record. Copies may be requested per Florida law and HIPAA (cost-based fee may apply).

Video Surveillance Notice

Non-disrobing areas are under video surveillance for safety. No surveillance in exam rooms, treatment bays where patients disrobe, or restrooms.

Consent to Evaluation and Treatment

I have read and understand the Terms of Acceptance above. I voluntarily consent to evaluation and treatment, may ask questions at any time, may refuse or discontinue treatment, and understand no outcome is guaranteed.
